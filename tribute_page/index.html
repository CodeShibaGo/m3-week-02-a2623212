Ruth Bader Ginsburg

She fought for women's rights as pioneering Supreme Court Justice.

Here's a time line of Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg's life:

  • 1933 - Born March 15 in Brooklyn, New York.
  • 1954 - Graduated from Cornell University. Married Martin Ginsburg.
  • 1956 - Daughter Jane born.
  • 1959 - Graduated first in class from Columbia Law School.
  • 1963 - Became assistant professor at Rutgers Law School.
  • 1970 - Became director of ACLU Women's Rights Project.
  • 1971 - Published law review articles on gender discrimination.
  • 1972 - Became the first tenured female faculty at Columbia Law.
  • 1973-1978 - Argued six cases before Supreme Court, winning five.
  • 1980 - Appointed to U.S. Court of Appeals by President Carter.
  • 1993 - Nominated to Supreme Court by President Clinton.
  • 1996 - Wrote majority opinion in United States v. Virginia.
  • 2009-2017 - Became progressive folk hero known as "Notorious R.B.G."
  • 2010 - Husband Martin passed away.
  • 2020 - Died September 18 at her home in Washington D.C. at age 87, having served as Supreme Court Justice for 27 years.
